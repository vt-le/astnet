Abstract

Automatic anomaly detection is an essential task in video surveillance system used for public safety. Many anomaly detection approaches used two-stream networks, which included a spatial stream and a temporal stream to exploit both spatial and temporal information from videos. The present study introduces an attention-based residual autoencoder for video anomaly detection. Features of video frames are exploited both spatial and temporal information in a single stream network. Furthermore, the contextual dependencies of features are exploited by spatial-channel attention. We evaluate the proposed DesNet on three benchmark datasets: USCD Ped2, CUHK Avenue and ShanghaiTech. Experiment results show that DesNet achieves the state-of-the-art performance with 97.4\%, 86.6\% and 73.6\% on USCD Ped2, CUHK Avenue and ShanghaiTech, respectively. The project page of this paper can be found at https://vt-le.github.io/desnet/.
